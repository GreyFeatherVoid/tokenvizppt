# Magazine Feature

## Color system
- Background: cream, off-white, or a bold editorial color field.
- Ink: rich black, espresso, or deep editorial tones.
- Accent: one strong spot color such as orange, red, cobalt, or moss.
- Surface: large crop-like blocks, pull-quote panels, and editorial frames.

## Visual character
Premium editorial spread. Slides should feel like designed magazine pages with rhythm, tension, and confident whitespace.

## Typography
Use oversized headlines, expressive serif-like titles, condensed labels, and sparse body text. Let typography become a visual object.

## Layout language
Use asymmetric spreads, edge-to-edge fields, dramatic title placement, pull quotes, image crops, and large negative space. Each slide should have an editorial hook.

## Data and shape language
Turn data into editorial callouts, ranking strips, annotation cards, or bold numbered statements rather than dashboard widgets.

## Best for
Brand stories, thought leadership, cultural analysis, product narratives, trend reports, and feature-like decks.

## Avoid
Small timid titles, dashboard grids, generic business cards, overly balanced templates, and too many equal-weight blocks.
