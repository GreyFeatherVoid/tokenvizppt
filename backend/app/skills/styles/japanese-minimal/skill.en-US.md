# Japanese Minimal

## Color system
- Background: warm washi paper, rice white, or pale beige.
- Ink: charcoal, sumi black, or muted brown-gray.
- Accent: muted vermilion, seal red, or dark moss.
- Surface: quiet panels, fine dividers, and seal-like marks.

## Visual character
Poetic, disciplined, calm, and asymmetrical. Clarity must come before atmosphere.

## Typography
Use refined serif-like titles or calm sans-serif. Keep body text small but readable. Let line breaks feel intentional.

## Layout language
Use disciplined asymmetry, large negative space, vertical side labels only when short, subtle dividers, and one meaningful accent mark.

## Data and shape language
Use understated tables, slim bars, simple step structures, and ink-like rules. Avoid noisy charts.

## Best for
Cultural topics, design, lifestyle, reflective strategy, premium products, education, and calm storytelling.

## Avoid
Loud gradients, heavy boxes, dense paragraphs, random Japanese decoration, and vertical text for long mixed-language titles.
