# Academic Paper

## Color system
- Background: paper white, warm gray, or muted parchment.
- Ink: charcoal, deep blue-gray, or academic black.
- Accent: restrained burgundy, muted blue, or olive for citations and highlights.
- Surface: paper cards, evidence blocks, and thin annotation rules.

## Visual character
Scholarly, precise, evidence-driven, and calm. The deck should feel like a research argument, not a sales pitch.

## Typography
Use serif-style headlines for authority and readable sans-serif body text. Use smaller labels and citation-like annotations sparingly.

## Layout language
Use hypothesis-method-evidence-implication structures, numbered claims, margin notes, evidence panels, and clean diagrams. Let logic define the layout.

## Data and shape language
Use clean tables, understated charts, brackets, annotation lines, and clear figure labels. Prefer clarity over visual drama.

## Best for
Research talks, policy analysis, academic summaries, technical papers, literature reviews, and evidence briefings.

## Avoid
Startup exaggeration, flashy gradients, decorative icons, unsupported claims, and crowded footnote-like text.
