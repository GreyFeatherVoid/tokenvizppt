# Data Studio

## Color system
- Background: cool white, very light gray, or muted analytical blue-gray.
- Ink: slate, charcoal, or deep blue-gray.
- Accent: restrained blue, teal, green, or orange for series and deltas.
- Surface: metric cards, table panels, comparison fields, and subtle chart frames.

## Visual character
Analytical, polished, evidence-first. It should look like an editorial analytics studio, not raw BI software.

## Typography
Use clean sans-serif hierarchy, compact labels, clear numbers, and stable alignment. Numerals should be prominent and readable.

## Layout language
Use metric rows, comparison grids, small multiples, ranking strips, evidence cards, and insight summaries. Do not overload a slide with every possible metric.

## Data and shape language
Use bar-like shapes, slope comparisons, mini tables, KPI chips, quadrant structures, and annotated evidence blocks. Make the takeaway visible before the detail.

## Best for
Business analysis, KPI review, market research, financial summaries, operating dashboards, and data storytelling.

## Avoid
Decorative layouts that weaken data readability, vague chart-like shapes, tiny labels, and unsupported exact values.
