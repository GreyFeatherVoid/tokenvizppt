# Executive Calm

## Color system
- Background: warm ivory or sand, optionally with a very soft green wash.
- Ink: deep forest green, charcoal, or dark slate.
- Accent: copper, muted terracotta, or antique gold used sparingly.
- Surface: off-white cards with thin warm gray borders.

## Visual character
Calm, boardroom-ready, evidence-first, and premium without looking luxurious. It should feel like a high-quality strategy memo translated into slides.

## Typography
Use elegant serif-like headlines when the slide needs authority, paired with clean sans-serif body text. Headlines should be confident but not huge. Body text should be compact, clear, and aligned.

## Layout language
Use asymmetric editorial grids, wide margins, compact evidence cards, executive summary bands, and strong metric blocks. Titles may sit top-left, side-left, or paired with a key number. Avoid center-heavy templates except cover or closing slides.

## Data and shape language
Use large numerals, KPI cards, muted comparison bars, thin dividers, and small label chips. Shapes should have subtle radius, light borders, and almost no shadow.

## Best for
Strategy reports, board updates, business reviews, financial summaries, product strategy, and leadership briefings.

## Avoid
Playful motifs, neon color, cartoon icons, dense dashboards, dramatic gradients, and overdecorated charts.
