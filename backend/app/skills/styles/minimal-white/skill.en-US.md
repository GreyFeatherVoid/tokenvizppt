# Minimal White

## Color system
- Background: pure white, porcelain, or very warm off-white.
- Ink: black, charcoal, or soft graphite.
- Accent: one tiny mark color only, such as red, blue, or olive.
- Surface: mostly no cards; when needed use hairline rules and very subtle panels.

## Visual character
Quiet, premium, exact. It should feel intentionally sparse, not unfinished.

## Typography
Use clean modern typography, strong size contrast, and careful line breaks. Every word must earn its place.

## Layout language
Use large margins, sparse alignment, small labels, strong focal statements, and one or two precise rules. Let absence create hierarchy.

## Data and shape language
Use minimal tables, single-number callouts, hairline dividers, and restrained annotations. Avoid decorative charts.

## Best for
Premium reports, design reviews, executive summaries, strategy statements, and content that benefits from restraint.

## Avoid
Gradients, shadows, heavy cards, decorative geometry, too many colors, and filling the page just because space exists.
