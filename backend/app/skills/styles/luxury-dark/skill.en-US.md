# Luxury Dark

## Color system
- Background: near-black, espresso, or deep midnight.
- Ink: ivory, pearl, or warm gray.
- Accent: champagne gold, bronze, or muted platinum.
- Surface: subtle dark panels, fine metallic rules, and premium frames.

## Visual character
Premium, cinematic, restrained, and high-value. It should feel expensive through spacing and restraint, not shiny effects.

## Typography
Use elegant serif-like headlines, refined sans-serif labels, and sparse body copy. Make fewer words feel more important.

## Layout language
Use dramatic negative space, side panels, gold rules, large hero statements, and carefully placed evidence cards. One strong focal area per page.

## Data and shape language
Use premium metric cards, slim gold dividers, dark comparison panels, and restrained callouts.

## Best for
Luxury brands, premium products, investor showcases, high-end reports, cinematic launches, and brand strategy.

## Avoid
Neon tech, cheap metallic gradients, busy dashboards, many colors, and cluttered card grids.
